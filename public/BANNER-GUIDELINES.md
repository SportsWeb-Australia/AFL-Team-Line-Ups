# Sponsor banner — design guidelines

How to make a banner in Canva (or anything) that renders sharp and readable on
every device, especially phones, inside the team line-up's rotating sponsor strip.

## The one rule that matters: design at 4:1

The strip is a wide, short band. The app shows the **whole** banner (it never
crops a normal banner), so the closer your artwork is to the strip's shape, the
bigger and cleaner it appears.

- **Canvas size: 1200 × 300 px (4:1).** This is the sweet spot.
- For extra crispness on high-res phones you can design at **2400 × 600** (same
  4:1) and export — just keep the file under ~300 KB.
- Avoid near-square or tall banners. They get capped in height and end up looking
  small and lost in the middle of the strip.
- Avoid ultra-wide strips (8:1 or wider, e.g. 1600 × 150). At that ratio the app
  switches to fill-and-crop and your edges get cut off.

## Make it readable at phone size

On a narrow phone the banner renders about as wide as the screen (~360 px) and
proportionally short (~90 px tall at 4:1). Design for *that* size, not your laptop.

- **Big, bold focal element** — the sponsor logo + a 3–5 word line. No paragraphs.
- **Minimum text height ~ 1/4 of the banner height.** If you can't read it on your
  own phone, neither can anyone else.
- **High contrast** — dark text on a light field or vice versa. Avoid thin grey
  type on a busy photo.
- **No fine print** (phone numbers, addresses, terms) — it's unreadable at this
  size. Put that on the sponsor's linked page instead.

## Layout & safe area

- Use a **full-bleed solid (or photographic) background** — the banner should read
  as a finished tile, not floating elements on transparent.
- Keep important content inside a **~5% margin** all the way around so nothing
  touches the edge.
- The app rounds the banner's corners for you, so **design flat** — don't add your
  own rounded corners or drop shadow.
- **Top-right corner:** the small "Advertise with us" tab now sits *straddling the
  top edge* above the strip, so it no longer covers artwork — but as a habit, don't
  bury a critical logo in the extreme top-right corner.

## File & export

- **PNG or JPG**, sRGB colour. PNG for logos/flat colour, JPG for photos.
- Export at the size above; keep it reasonably small (a few hundred KB) so the
  page stays fast on mobile data.
- Name the file clearly (`workplace-alliance-1200x300.png`) so it's easy to manage.

## Using multiple sponsors

Each sponsor is its **own banner slot** — the strip rotates through them every few
seconds. Don't cram several logos onto one banner; add separate banners and let
them cycle. Each can carry its own click-through link.

## Quick checklist before you upload

- [ ] 4:1 ratio (1200 × 300 or 2400 × 600)
- [ ] Logo + short line, readable on your phone at arm's length
- [ ] Solid/full-bleed background, flat corners
- [ ] Content inside a 5% margin
- [ ] Under ~300 KB, PNG/JPG, sRGB
- [ ] Previewed on an actual phone
